About Me

I am Hang Dai, a sophomore at School of EECS, Peking University majoring in Information and Computing Science.

My research focuses on cutting-edge topics in robotics and computer vision, including in-hand object reconstruction and 3D manipulation. I am passionate about bridging theoretical advances with practical applications, and I strive to balance rigorous academic coursework, engaging research projects, and meaningful social work. Always eager to learn and collaborate, I welcome discussions on innovative technologies and interdisciplinary initiatives.

My Work & Research

  • In-Hand Object Reconstruction

    Developing methods for reconstructing and manipulating objects in hand for robotic applications.

  • Real2Sim2Real

    Bridging the Sim2Real gap in robotic manipulation.

  • Computer Vision for Robotics

    Enhancing visual perception for robots to understand and interact with their environments more effectively.
